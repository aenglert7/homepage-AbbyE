My Reptile Friends!

Over the past few years I have learned to love and care for 8 differnt species of reptiles. Including a lepard gecko, crested gecko, chinese cave gecko, african fat-tailed gecko, gargoyl gecko, chahoua gecko, veiled chameleon, and a hog nose snake.

Now lets meet my Friends!

Sunny

Sunny is a Lepard Gecko!
She is a dessert gecko and/or a sand dweller. That means she like warm dry areas. She is currently livng in a 40 gallon tank with a warm hide, cool hide, and a humidity hide. She also has her large rock wall to climb on. Sunny was my very first reptile and she is the oldest of the bunch! Lepard geos patterns very but they get their name from the black spots on their bodies. they also usally have some kinf of yellow or tan surrounding them.I feed her chrickets and meal worms.



Moon

He is a African Fat-tailed Gecko!
Moon is a boy and my newest reptile! He is very similar to my lepard geck sunny when it comes to looks. He just has more browns and whites in his pattern. Now when it comes to care he has the same three hids but requires a higher humity throuhgout the tank. Meaning he needs to be misted at least twice daily. He is also living in a 40 gallon tank! I feed him crickets and mealworms.



Shade

He is a Chinese Cave Gecko!
Sade also looks similar to the african fat-tailed gecko and the lepard gecko. He is nrown with yellow strips and is quite a bit smaller then the other geckos when fully grown. he is currently in a 10 gallon still growing to eventually be in his 20 galloon tank. He does not require a heat source only UVB. He also requires a higher humity so missing his tank twice a day! I mainly feed him crickets becasue he's picky.



Rayn & Rocky

These two are both Crested Geckos! Rayn & Rocky!
Rayn is the older of the two and the more friendly one haha. Rocky is just very shy and scared all the time. These geckos are considered tropical. That means they need to be misted and don't really need a heat source. Just a heating pad on the side of the glass if they get cold. These guys are very jumpy and have sticky feet so they can climb glass. These guys are very goofy and need a vertical enclosure. I belive I have them in 30 gallon terrariums right now. They aslo can drop their tails and it wont grow back. The only other thing is they eat crickets, meal worms, and a powder food mix.



Flame

He is a Gargoyle Gecko!
He is related to the crested gecko. They are both from new calidonia. He also has sticky feet and jumps, but not as much. He needs the same type of enviroment and also needs to be fed the same diet. He preffers the powered food though haha. These guys can also be a little bit agressive at times.



Branch

He is a Chahoua Gecko!
Branch is also related to the crested and gargoyl gecko. He gets a little bit bigger then them though. He also preffers a more bug diet. Same environment as well.



Rango

He is a Veiled Chameleon! This is my only reptile that does not like me hahaha. Ithink he mainly doesnt rust me, but he is very smart. These chameleons are the most common. The males have more patterns then the femals. he in currently living in a mesh enclosure with a misting system and waterfall for moving water to drink from. He mainly eats crickets but also eats mealworms and horn worms.



Henry

He is a Hog Nose Snake! These snakes do not get very large about 3feet max. The females usally get larger then the males. He is currently living in a 20gallon soon to be upgraded to his 30 gallon. The species of snake loves to dig. That is why his nose points up to dig in the soil. This spiecs ussaly eats frongs in the wild. I am currently feeding him two pinkys once a week.
